<p><p>The porcine vaccine market comprises several types: Inactivated vaccines, which use killed pathogens to induce immunity; live attenuated vaccines, featuring weakened strains for a strong immune response; toxoid vaccines, made from inactivated toxins to protect against toxin-producing bacteria; recombinant vaccines, utilizing genetically engineered microorganisms; conjugate vaccines, linking polysaccharides to proteins for enhanced immunity; and DNA vaccines, employing genetically engineered DNA to provoke a protective immune response. Each type addresses specific diseases and enhances swine health and productivity.</p></p>
