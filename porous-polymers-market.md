<p><p>The porous polymers market can be divided into two primary types based on their structural states: crystalline and amorphous. Crystalline porous polymers exhibit a well-ordered arrangement of molecules, leading to distinct pore structures, optimal for applications requiring specific strength and stability. In contrast, amorphous porous polymers feature a random molecular arrangement, resulting in greater flexibility and enhanced sorption properties. Both types serve critical roles in various applications, including filtration, drug delivery, and environmental remediation, catering to diverse industry needs.</p></p>
