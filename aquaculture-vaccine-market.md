<p><p>The aquaculture vaccine market comprises several types, primarily categorized into live vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and other vaccines. Live vaccines use attenuated pathogens to stimulate immune responses, providing long-lasting protection. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, offering safety and stability but often requiring boosters. Other vaccines include subunit, DNA, and mRNA vaccines, utilizing specific components of pathogens to elicit an immune response, potentially providing innovative solutions for disease control in aquaculture. Each type plays a crucial role in maintaining fish health and productivity.</p></p>
