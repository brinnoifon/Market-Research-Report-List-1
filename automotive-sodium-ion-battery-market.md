<p><p>The automotive sodium-ion battery market includes various types, notably sodium-salt (Zebra) batteries and sodium-sulfur batteries. Sodium-salt batteries, known for their high energy density and safety, utilize a liquid sodium chloride electrolyte, making them suitable for electric vehicles. In contrast, sodium-sulfur batteries operate at elevated temperatures and offer high capacity and efficiency, making them ideal for stationary energy storage and grid applications. Both types provide environmentally friendly alternatives to conventional lithium-ion batteries, potentially reducing reliance on scarce materials.</p></p>
